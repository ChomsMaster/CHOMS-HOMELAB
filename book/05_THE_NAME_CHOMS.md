# The Name: CHOMS

## Every project deserves an identity

Most engineering projects receive a name for practical reasons.

A repository needs a title.

A company needs a brand.

A product requires an identity.

CHOMS was different.

The name was never created as part of a marketing exercise.

It appeared naturally.

Only afterwards did it become part of something much larger.

---

# A personal origin

CHOMS is an acronym built from my own name.

**CH** comes from **Chirinos**.

**O** comes from **Oscar**.

**M** comes from **Manuel**.

**S** comes from **Salcedo**.

Together they form CHOMS.

There is no hidden technical meaning.

No complex branding strategy.

No marketing agency.

Simply my own identity condensed into four letters.

At the time, I never imagined those four letters would eventually represent an entire engineering platform.

---

# Why "Master"?

The second part of the original name became **ChomsMaster**.

The word "Master" was never intended to imply superiority over other people.

It represented something much simpler.

A personal aspiration.

Mastery.

The continuous pursuit of becoming better every year.

Better as an engineer.

Better as a software architect.

Better as a systems designer.

Better as a professional.

The name therefore became a reminder rather than a title.

A reminder that learning never ends.

---

# From a personal brand to an engineering platform

Originally, ChomsMaster was simply the name used for my personal website.

Later it became associated with my professional identity.

Eventually it also became the home of my engineering projects.

When the Homelab initiative began, using the same name was an obvious decision.

At first, the infrastructure was known simply as **CHOMS-HOMELAB**.

The name accurately described the project at that stage.

It was a Homelab.

Nothing more.

However, as the architecture evolved, something changed.

The Homelab was no longer the objective.

It had become only one component of a much larger vision.

---

# Beyond the Homelab

As new capabilities were planned, the project expanded far beyond personal infrastructure.

Future versions would support:

- Public web services.
- Backend APIs.
- Distributed applications.
- Monitoring platforms.
- VPN services.
- Private cloud infrastructure.
- Educational systems.
- Automation platforms.
- Development environments.
- Future Kubernetes clusters.
- AI experimentation.
- Enterprise operational practices.

Calling the entire initiative a Homelab no longer reflected reality.

The infrastructure had evolved into a platform.

---

# The evolution of the name

The progression can be described as follows:

ShiftCore
↓

Personal VPN Infrastructure
↓

CHOMS-HOMELAB

↓

Engineering Infrastructure

↓

Private Cloud Platform

↓

CHOMS Platform

Each stage represents an increase in architectural maturity rather than a complete replacement.

Nothing was discarded.

Each phase became the foundation for the next one.

---

# Identity matters

The name CHOMS is intentionally preserved across every stage of the journey.

Although technologies will inevitably change, the identity remains constant.

It represents continuity.

Experience.

Growth.

Engineering discipline.

Long-term thinking.

The platform may eventually span multiple servers, clusters, geographic locations and services.

Its identity, however, will continue to originate from the same four letters that started everything.

CHOMS.

A simple name.

A personal beginning.

The foundation of an engineering platform designed to evolve for many years to come.
