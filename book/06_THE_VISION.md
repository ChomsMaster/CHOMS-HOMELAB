# The Vision

## Looking beyond today's infrastructure

CHOMS Platform is not being designed for today's requirements.

It is being designed for the next decade.

Every engineering decision taken throughout this project attempts to answer a simple question:

*"Will this decision still make sense several years from now?"*

If the answer is no, the decision is reconsidered.

The platform is intentionally evolving more slowly than many similar projects because long-term sustainability has priority over short-term progress.

The objective has never been to deploy as many services as possible.

The objective is to build an engineering ecosystem capable of supporting future projects without requiring fundamental redesign.

---

# The platform is the product

Many Homelab projects focus on the services being deployed.

Docker.

Kubernetes.

Nextcloud.

Jellyfin.

Pi-hole.

Grafana.

While all of these technologies are valuable, none of them represents the actual product.

Within CHOMS Platform the infrastructure itself is not the final objective.

It is the foundation upon which future products will be built.

The real product is the platform.

Infrastructure exists to provide reliable services.

Reliable services exist to support applications.

Applications exist to solve real problems.

The platform therefore becomes an enabling layer capable of supporting multiple independent initiatives simultaneously.

---

# An ecosystem instead of isolated projects

One of the earliest architectural decisions was to avoid building isolated systems.

Instead, every new component should contribute to a growing ecosystem.

Today that ecosystem already includes multiple long-term initiatives.

Among them are:

• CHOMS Platform infrastructure.

• ChomsMaster public website.

• ShiftCore orchestration platform.

• Private VPN infrastructure connecting Spain and Venezuela.

• Personal private cloud.

• Media services.

• Monitoring and observability.

• Centralised authentication.

• Secure remote administration.

These services already benefit from sharing common infrastructure.

Future projects will continue following the same philosophy.

# Supporting real-world projects

The platform is not being built only for experimentation.

Several future projects already depend on its existence.

Among them are:

## ChomsMaster

The complete public website will eventually operate entirely from this infrastructure.

The objective is to own every layer involved in delivering the service.

Hosting.

Networking.

Security.

Monitoring.

Backups.

Deployment.

Everything.

---

## ShiftCore

ShiftCore represents one of the largest software initiatives planned for the coming years.

Its backend services, APIs, databases and supporting infrastructure are expected to run on CHOMS Platform.

Rather than depending entirely on third-party cloud providers, ShiftCore will initially be capable of operating using infrastructure fully controlled by the project itself.

This creates complete freedom to experiment, optimise and evolve the architecture.

# Growth through phases

One of the defining characteristics of CHOMS Platform is its phased evolution.

Large engineering programmes are rarely built in a single iteration.

Each completed phase creates a stable baseline for future work.

The project therefore follows an incremental engineering model.

Phase 1 established the foundation.

Future phases will progressively introduce:

Additional nodes.

Centralised storage.

Cluster management.

Virtualization.

Container orchestration.

Automation.

Observability.

High availability.

Distributed services.

Artificial Intelligence.

Enterprise operational practices.

Each phase increases the maturity of the platform without compromising previous achievements.

---

# Enterprise thinking on commodity hardware

Perhaps the most unusual characteristic of CHOMS Platform is that enterprise engineering principles are intentionally applied to inexpensive hardware.

The objective is not to simulate an enterprise.

The objective is to demonstrate that good engineering is independent of budget.

Professional architecture does not begin with expensive servers.

It begins with disciplined decision making.

A well-designed platform built on modest hardware often demonstrates greater engineering capability than an expensive infrastructure assembled without architectural discipline.

This philosophy influences every technical decision made throughout the project.

---

# Measuring success

The success of CHOMS Platform will never be measured by the number of containers running.

Nor by the amount of hardware installed.

Nor by storage capacity.

Success will instead be measured by questions such as:

Can the platform evolve without major redesign?

Can services be replaced independently?

Can failures be recovered predictably?

Can another engineer understand the architecture?

Can future projects be integrated naturally?

Can operational complexity remain under control as the platform grows?

If the answer to those questions remains positive, the project is succeeding.

---

# A ten-year commitment

CHOMS Platform is not expected to be completed next month.

Nor next year.

It is expected to evolve continuously for many years.

Technologies will change.

Hardware will be replaced.

Architectures will mature.

New ideas will emerge.

Some decisions will inevitably prove incorrect.

Others will survive for a decade.

Every one of those changes will become part of the documented engineering journey.

The objective is simple.

To build an engineering platform that continues growing long after the first server was powered on.

The Homelab was never the destination.

It was only the first step.
